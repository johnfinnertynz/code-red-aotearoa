Local action alerts

Turn the evidence into pressure where you live.

This starter sheet gives the campaign a rhythm: watch local signals, act quickly, and keep the demand focused on measurable public healthcare recovery.

Watch For

  • Emergency department delays, ambulance ramping, or wards operating over capacity.
  • Hospital service reductions, hiring freezes, vacancy spikes, or roster gaps.
  • Local aged-care closures, bed shortages, dementia-care waits, or home-support cuts.
  • Private outsourcing announcements that do not publish cost, outcomes, or workforce impacts.
  • Mental-health referral thresholds rising, services closing books, or long private waitlists.
  • Local court, Police, or prison stories involving mental-health crisis where earlier care was absent.

Act Within 24 Hours

  1. Save the article, report, OIA, or firsthand account.
  2. Write a two-sentence local summary: what happened, who is affected, what system failure it shows.
  3. Send the MP email template to your electorate MP and one relevant minister.
  4. Send the same issue to local media with the subject line: "Local healthcare failure needs answers".
  5. Share the evidence pack with one community group, union branch, school board, or aged-care network.

Message Discipline

Keep every alert tied to the same test: need is rising, access is narrowing, staff are unsafe, and vulnerable people are being pushed downstream. Ask for public reporting, safe staffing, workforce retention, mental-health access, aged-care capacity, and transparent private-contract data.

Local Alert Template

Headline: [Local service/patient/staffing issue] shows healthcare failure in [place]

What happened: [One or two factual sentences with source link.]

Why it matters: This is part of the wider pattern: rising need, narrowed access, unsafe staffing, and pressure being shifted onto patients, families, staff, aged care, and justice services.

Ask: Publish the local recovery plan, staffing numbers, wait times, outsourcing costs, and patient outcome data.
